About Subhas Chandra Bose

Subhas Chandra Bose, affectionately known as Netaji, was one of the most prominent leaders of India's freedom struggle. Born on January 23, 1897, in Cuttack, Odisha, he was a patriot, visionary, and a courageous fighter who inspired millions to fight for India's independence from British rule.

Bose is well known for his leadership of the Indian National Army (INA) and his call for unity and determination in the struggle for freedom. His famous slogan, "Give me blood, and I shall give you freedom!" continues to inspire generations.

Key Achievements

  • Leader of the Indian National Army (INA).
  • Formed the Azad Hind Government.
  • Renowned for his strategic and military contributions to India's independence movement.
